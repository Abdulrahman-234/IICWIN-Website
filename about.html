About Us

Welcome to IICWIN

Who we are?

Initiative for Integrated Community Welfare in Nigeria (IICWIN) is a community-based, people-focused organization, contributing to transforming Nigeria into a nation of empowered, healthy, and happy Communities. IICWIN has a heart for the people.

Philosophy

Individual total well-being and happiness are only sustainable in the wider context of community happiness and prosperity.

Vision

To be a world-class community-oriented and people-focused organization contributing to transforming Nigeria to a nation of empowered, healthy, and happy communities free from the scourges of disease, poverty, and ignorance.

Mission

IICWIN passionately seeks to add value to Nigerian communities by delivering quality people-focused and high-impact interventions that meet their health and related development needs with emphasis on the vulnerable and the underserved.

IICWIN Core Values

Passion: We are deeply committed to the well-being of Nigerian communities and peoples

Excellence: We strive to achieve the highest quality in our work

Teamwork: We work together to enhance our performance and obtain better results

Integrity: Transparency, accountability, honesty and integrity drive our programs and our finances.

Respect: We respect all (employees and beneficiaries) and embrace and value diversity.

Innovations: Identifying new and novel practices to leverage the opportunities that exist.

Knowledge and Competence: Our service interventions are evidence based.

IICWIN Objectives

1.To provide accurate information on all aspects of public health to mobilize and sensitize the community, with the aim of fighting ignorance, disease, and poverty.

2. To provide access to comprehensive community-based health and protection services for women, children, adolescents and youths.

3. To strengthen community capacity by utilizing existing structures to respond to endemic and emerging health, poverty, and development issues.

4. To develop a local community-based response to the growing needs of the vulnerable children, women, families, and other vulnerable groups in the under-served communities.

5.To collaborate with other stakeholders for coordinated responses to maximize the effect of interventions in communities.

Our Accomplishments

Over 20 Years of Accomplishments

Supported 12,000 vulnerable children in over 4,000 rural ad urban households with psychosocial, nutrition, health, education, shelter, and protection services.
Over 1,000 caregivers in vulnerable families empowered with education and livelihood support.


About 122 community caregivers received training in vulnerable children care and support.


Over 250,000 community members reached with FGM education and awareness.


50 community FGM champions trained.


Public declaration against FGM in 4 communities with certificate of declaration and billboards declaring the communities FGM-free.
