ADOLESCENT GIRLS PROGRAM

Sessions with Female Adolescent Youth Mentoring Program. Mentoring on values & self esteem “I am more”: Adolescent Girls were mentored on not underestimating their abilities in achieving great heights as females. They were engaged on looking inwards to achieve greatness. Emphasized the following values: DIGNITY, SELF-ESTEEM, POSITIVE CORE VALUES – HONESTY, INTEGRITY, ASPIRATION, AMBITION, ENTHUSIASM Mentoring on Dress sense and clothing skill: The session was used to expose girls to decent dress sense & clothing skill. This was used to explain Gender Based Violence (GBV) to them and guide them to protect their dignity. DRESS SENSE AS A TOOL FOR i) PROTECTING THEIR DIGNITY, SELF ESTEEM, AND SENSE OF DECENCY AND ii) AVOIDANCE OF GBV Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics (STEM) Mentoring Program for Young girls Empowering Young girls for STEM through Collaborating and Partnering & Reaching out to catch them young The rationale is to introduce the female school-going youths to STEM ideas and engagement activities that are not always available in their homes and schools. This is to facilitate the girls with skills and tools for entering into STEM fields. This is in line with SDG 4 (Gender Equality) as hitherto, STEM has been dominated by the male gender. a.) Celebrating the International Day of the Girl Child with STEM Program Appreciation To contribute to achieving this, IICWIN implemented a 1-Day STEM Appreciation Program on the International Day of the Girl Child 2021 whose theme is Digital Generation, Our Generation. The objectives were to: i. Reach out to 20 underserved youths (girls) with respect to STEM program. ii. Sensitize young girls on knowledge and skills that will build their esteem, allow them work as a team to set up projects. iii. Incentivize girls to become involved in technology. iv. Mathematical capacity building for under-served girls  Enhance cooperation and partnership with other networks and organizations such as: CPN, TECHBRIDGE, Akinyele LGA, State Education Department, etc (L.I.E) The aspects covered included: Robotics, Microbits, Cyber Security, The outputs of the program were:  Twenty-one (21) under-served girls provided with opportunity of exposure to STEM and Robotics/Programming.  Participants gained knowledge STEM and Robotics as an assessment of knowledge carried out at the beginning of the program showed only 2% had basic prior knowledge of STEM while Post-Test showed 90% had basic knowledge of STEM and Robotics. b.) Collaboration for Community Outreach on IDGC Day for STEM sensitization/awareness As an outreach to the community on IDGC Day, IICWIN actively participated in Oyo State Child Protection Network STEM Outreach to girls in Ido LGA secondary schools with 89 students in attendance. WOMEN EMPOWERMENT PROGRAM

The Women Program aims to provide access to health, economic, and social empowerment for women’s resilience and overall wellbeing as mothers to youth and children. This is in line with our integrated approach to health and development. Some aspects of child and youth care and wellbeing are incorporated into women’s activities and learning as we believe the well-being of children and youth starts from home, with mothers being critically important. The strategy is to employ women’s economic empowerment and self-help groups as vehicle for implementation of programs for their health and issues to do with their children and youth. This integrates health promotion and education activities into the group meetings in order to promote the health and wellbeing of the urban and rural under-served women and their children and youth. Livelihood/Economic Empowerment Initially meant for the support of HIV-affected families, the program gradually expanded to reach other urban and rural active poor women. The group savings and loan program serves as channel to reach the women with health and development interventions for themselves, children and families Along with business management training and small revolving loans for business development and strengthening, over 3000 women received empowerment to enhance livelihood and achieve incomes generation for themselves and their families. It has been extended to support groups of rural farmers and business owners. The Women enrolled in the empowerment program are equipped with health information and messages aimed at a long-term behavioral change for positive living. Women’s Health/Wellness It is vital to prevent and control communicable and non-communicable diseases among women to improve their general wellbeing. Women often live with unaddressed illnesses such as hypertension, diabetes and other non-communicable diseases as they often are too preoccupied with their economic, gender and reproductive roles. IICWIN conducted the Project ‘Know your blood pressure and diabetes status’. This was a survey of non-communicable diseases among the women members of IICWIN Health and Wealth Groups. The occurrence of undiagnosed Hypertension was high among the 211 assessed women (20.9%). High blood sugar was found among 1.9% while high BP and high blood sugar coexisted among 2.4%. This was a life-saving project that brought unknown hypertension and diabetes to light. Sexual and Gender Based Violence Sexual and Gender-based violence has increased phenomenally • Rape and Sexual Violence • Female Genital Mutilation • Women and Youth Trafficking and Irregular migration • Other forms of Violence . We implement the following activities for women and young who face SGBV • Awareness and education on gender-based violence for the women and young in outreaches, on radio program, social media. • Popularization and dissemination of the Oyo State Violence Against People Prohibition VAPP Law 2020 • Response to reported cases of GBV in partnership with relevant stakeholders. This consists of rescue followed by health, psycho-social and legal support for women survivors of SGBV.
